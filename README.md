## Project Overview

This project analyzes Spotify streaming data across the Top 100 tracks, covering artist performance, track popularity, playlist reach, streaming trends by release year, and platform comparisons between Spotify and YouTube. The dashboard surfaces what drives streams — track score, explicit content ratio, playlist inclusion, and artist catalog depth.

## Key Findings

**1. Cover versions dominate the top streams — Danza Kuduro Cover (21bn) and Cake By The Ocean Cover (15bn)**
Surprisingly, cover versions outperform original tracks at the top. This reflects algorithmic amplification of tracks that accumulate streams across multiple versions and listener discovery patterns.

**2. 25% of Top 100 tracks are explicit but only drive 23.73% of streams**
Explicit content is slightly underperforming its representation — non-explicit tracks generate proportionally more streams per track, suggesting broader audience accessibility drives higher play counts.

**3. Bad Bunny and The Weeknd are tied at 37bn streams each**
Both artists demonstrate sustained global streaming dominance across multiple years and genres (Latin trap vs R&B/pop) — indicating multi-market appeal is key to top-tier streaming performance.

**4. Ed Sheeran, Olivia Rodrigo, and The Weeknd each have 4 tracks in the Top 100**
Catalog depth in the Top 100 signals consistent hit-making ability — these artists don't rely on a single viral track but maintain multiple simultaneously popular songs.

**5. 2024 releases outperform 2023 releases in most months**
The streaming trend comparison shows 2024 tracks consistently generating higher monthly streams than equivalent 2023 releases — reflecting shorter time-to-stream cycles as release strategies evolve.

**6. Spotify (52.84%) narrowly leads YouTube (47.16%) in platform share**
The near-even split suggests music consumption is genuinely bifurcated between audio-first (Spotify) and video-first (YouTube) platforms, with neither achieving dominant market capture.
